Juan,
 
    If I tell you to move your robot to a certain position and orientation, I would normally do this in terms of the end effector with respect to the station frame.  You would first premultiply by Tbase-wrt-station and postmultiply by Tendeffector-wrt-6 to get T06 for the specific position and orientation that I have asked you to move to.  I might give you this information in terms of roll-pitch-yaw for the orientation (r values) and px, py, pz for position.  You would convert the roll-pitch-yaw to a nine element r matrix and combine it with the p vector to get the 4 X 4 homogeneous transformation matrix for the particular position and orientation that i have ased you to move to.  Once you get there, I may give you the next position and orientation to move to, etc.  For any one specified position and orientation, the r's and p's are fixed for your inverse kinematics solution.

Juan,
 
    All of the r values, i.e., r13, r23, r33, etc., as well as the position vector px, py, pz, are assumed known for the inverse kinematics problem.  That is, you know all of the values of the T06 matrix, which consists of the nine r values and the p vector.  Knowing these, the problem is to solve for the thetas, 1-6, as a function of the r values, p values and link parameters, i.e., a's, alpha's, d's, etc.
    The values you give for the r values are the forward kinematics.  The inverse kinematics problem is to assume the r's known and solve for the thetas on the right hand side.
 
    G. Cook
